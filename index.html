Common questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Avodah decide loan or insurance approval?

No. Avodah can help you understand options, prepare requirements, and ask the right questions, but final approval, underwriting, eligibility, pricing, and availability are decided by the relevant provider or institution.

What information should I prepare before a consultation?

Prepare your main goal, basic contact details, employment or business background, estimated income or budget, existing insurance or financial commitments, and any documents related to the service you want to discuss.

Do I need to be in the Philippines to inquire?

No. You can inquire even if you are overseas. Avodah can discuss available communication options and practical next steps based on your location and situation.

Can OFWs and seafarers request guidance?

Yes. Avodah supports OFWs, seafarers, and their families with guidance for protection, money management, opportunities, and planning decisions.

Is the first conversation a sales call or an assessment?

It is a guidance and assessment conversation. Avodah first listens to your goals and situation, then discusses possible next steps when a service or solution appears suitable.

Is it via phone call or in-person?

Avodah prefers in-person conversations when practical, but phone and video calls are available for clients who are overseas, onboard, traveling, or unable to meet in person.

Do I need to pay for a consultation?

No. The initial consultation is completely free.
